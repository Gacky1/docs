## Bot Permissions

For Gacky-X to effectively protect your server, it requires several high-level permissions. We recommend granting the **Administrator** permission to ensure the Antinuke system can manage channels and roles during an emergency.

### Required Permissions Table

| Permission | Reason |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Manage Roles** | Required to restore roles, quarantine users, and assign autoroles. |
| **Manage Channels** | Required to lock down the server and restore deleted channels. |
| **Ban Members** | Required for the Antinuke to stop malicious actors. |
| **Kick Members** | Required for Anti-Bot and Anti-Raid protections. |
| **View Audit Log** | **CRITICAL:** Used to identify the executors of "nuke" actions. |
| **Manage Messages** | Required for `-purge` and deleting spam/scam links. |
| **Moderate Members** | Required for AI-based and Automod timeouts. |

## Role Hierarchy

In Discord, no bot can moderate a user who has a role **equal to or higher** than the bot's own highest role.

<Warning>
  **Move Gacky-X to the Top:** To ensure 100% security, go to **Server Settings > Roles** and drag the **Gacky-X** role to the very top. If Gacky-X is below your staff roles, it **cannot** punish them if their account is compromised.
</Warning>

## Access Tiers

Gacky-X uses a custom internal hierarchy to manage who can control its settings:

### 1. Primary Server Owner
The person who created the server. They have **Total Control** and can never be punished by the bot. They are the only ones who can manage **Extra Owners**.

### 2. Extra Owners (`-extraowner`)
The most trusted co-owners. They bypass all AutoMod filters and can manage **Security Admins**. They are still monitored by Antinuke but can be whitelisted for specific actions.

### 3. Security Admins (`-admin`)
Staff members who can configure the bot (`-setup`, `-automod`, etc.). They bypass AutoMod filters but are strictly monitored by the Antinuke system.

### 4. Whitelisted Users/Roles (`-whitelist`)
Trusted entities who have been given permission to perform specific actions (like "Channel Deletion") that would normally trigger the Antinuke.

<Tip>
  Use the **`-whitelist`** grid to grant granular permissions instead of whitelisting someone for everything.
</Tip>
